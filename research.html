Research Statement

Toward a clearer picture of regulatory and sequence drivers of evolution

My work compares sex‑bias, tissue specificity, and expression levels across eight Drosophila genomes to understand how regulatory programs shape molecular evolution. I integrate comparative RNA‑seq, codon usage (CAI), and genomic context to model drivers of dN/dS variation, using GLMs and phylogenetic models (EVE; HyPhy aBSREL/BUSTED).

Ongoing directions include scaling species‑specific codon reference sets, adding robust uncertainty estimates for tau thresholds, and formalizing cross‑species generalization via hierarchical EVE fits. Ultimately, I aim to connect gene regulation to evolutionary outcomes in a way that supports practical design—like CRISPR HDR arm selection grounded in conserved structure.

Recent highlights

  • GLMs across ~10k orthologs suggest sex‑bias and tissue specificity are significant predictors of rate.
  • Head‑expressed genes show CDS length as an additional contributor.
  • PCA/UMAP show strong clustering by tissue across species, validating data quality.
